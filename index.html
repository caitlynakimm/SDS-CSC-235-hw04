Network Analysis of the Top 15 Most Common Nouns and Adjectives

in Charles Dicken's Novel David Copperfield


  • This tool analyzes Charles Dickens's writing style in his novel David Copperfield by examining the relationships between the 15 most common nouns and the 15 most common adjectives in the story.
  • Each node represents a word, and each edge represents the two words appearing in the same sentence.
  • Drag nodes to change the network's structure and uncover cliques or patterns in Dickens's writing, such as which nouns and adjectives tend to be paired together.
  • Click a node to see a breakdown of how many nouns and adjectives that word is connected to, revealing which type of term is more frequently paired with it.
